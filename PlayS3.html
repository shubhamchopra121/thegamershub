Review

You can't accuse the 'new' super-slim PS3 of not living up to its (unofficial) name. It's a compact workhorse ready to bestow all the goodies of previous PS3s in 20% less space. It's still a great Blu-ray playback device, and as Sony extends its cross-play functionality with the Vita, they keep chucking in quality titles for PS Plus members and adding new and classic content to the PSN, it's going from strength to strength as a games console. And the SSD in the 12GB model is a really enticing prospect. We'd expect the next round of consoles to use exclusively solid state storage devices, so here's a chance to step into tomorrow's world. Although it is around 10 years old, it is still a great product for the budget gamer or even for just a entertainment centre as it has all the features and apps you could need. The playstation network is forever expanding so you can have access to new games and apps via their store. So realistically, you're looking at the cheapest entertainment centre which provides games, apps, movies and catch up TV with a decent amount of storage (Model dependent).
